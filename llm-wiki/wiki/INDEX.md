## Topics

- [[topics/circle-of-competence]] — The set of businesses an investor genuinely understands; size doesn't matter, knowing the boundary does.
- [[topics/cycles]] — Economies, profits, credit, psychology and prices oscillate around a midpoint they rarely occupy; calibrate, don't forecast.
- [[topics/drawdowns-and-temperament]] — Volatility as the price of admission; risk attitudes cycle; the greatest risk is the belief there is no risk.
- [[topics/intrinsic-value]] — The present-value estimate of cash a business can return to owners during its remaining life; anchor for all of value investing.
- [[topics/margin-of-safety]] — Buy below intrinsic value by a wide enough margin that errors of judgement leave you whole.
- [[topics/mr-market-and-temperament]] — Graham's manic-depressive partner; Marks's pendulum; exploit, don't predict.
- [[topics/owner-earnings]] — Cash a business can return to owners without impairing competitive position; the 1986 Scott Fetzer Appendix formula.
- [[topics/position-concentration]] — A small number of high-conviction positions; Buffett's "20-hole punch card" and "Secret Sauce".
- [[topics/second-level-thinking]] — Price the divergence between consensus and reality, not the consensus itself.
- [[topics/wonderful-businesses-and-time]] — Business quality dominates entry price over a long horizon; the Munger-shifted Buffett doctrine.
